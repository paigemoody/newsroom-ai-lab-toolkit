# What we do

## Figure out what's going on for real people

Your first task is to capture gut-level observations before we dig deeper.

The goal is to connect with as many 'users' as possible to capture as many of their real-world experiences as possible, in a structured format.

Users in the newsroom case might be folks internal to your org (think: reporters, editors, audience strategists, etc) as well as those you serve - readers, partners, etc. 

You will do your best to capture what you hear - without worring about perfect framing! 

Think of this step like the background research you do when starting a new reporting project.
    
## Format



## Categorize each experience into one of the three broad categories

Here are some prompts to help get people thinking:

**😣Pain points** – frictions you feel or see   
    - *In the last week, the moments that felt most broken were…*  
    - *When I try to… what often happens that gets in the way? Why?*  
    - *The hardest / most frustrating part of… is …? Give a recent example.*  
    - *We keep getting tripped up by…? How often? Who feels it most?*


**🐢Inefficiencies** – time sinks, brittle processes, or repetitive steps  
    - Tasks that took longer than they "should" have  
    - *I spend \_\_\_ hours/days doing \_\_\_\_\_ because\_\_\_\_\_.*  
    - *This step is manual/repetitive: \_\_\_\_\_. What’s the impact if it fails?*  
    - *We re-enter/copy the same info when \_\_\_\_\_ happens. What’s the consequence?*


**🧙Aspirational capabilities** – the "if we had a magic wand …" stuff

    - If you could change or create something new, what would it be and why?  
    - *If we could \[do/make/have\] \_\_\_\_\_, our team/readers could \[benefit\] \_\_\_\_\_.*
    - *I wish we understood \_\_\_\_\_ about our workflow/colleagues/readers because \_\_\_\_\_.*  
    - A capability we don’t have but want

#### Tips:

* Remember - the empathize stage is not about figuring out what’s possible — it is about **getting information about what might be worth exploring.**  
* **Be specific** — include who’s involved, where/when it happens, and what the impact is.  
* **Include both problems and opportunities** — frustrations, slowdowns, but also exciting "what if" ideas. Really emphasize the 🪄 magic wand 🪄 framing! 
* **No solutions** (yet!) — If a solution pops into your head, drop it into the separate ***Ideas Parking Lot*** section at the far right of the spreadsheet so you don’t lose it. After making note, pivot back to focus on *describing* the problem or opportunity in detail.
